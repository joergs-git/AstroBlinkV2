# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** March 7, 2026
**Developer:** joergsflow
**Contact:** joergsflow@gmail.com

This privacy policy applies to the following apps by joergsflow:

- **AstroBlinkV2** (macOS)
- **AstroFileViewer** (iOS / iPadOS)

---

## Summary

**We do not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. Period.**

Both apps run entirely on your device. There are no servers, no analytics, no tracking, no accounts, and no internet connection required.

---

## Data Collection

**None.** Our apps do not collect any data of any kind, including but not limited to:

- No personal information (name, email, phone number)
- No device identifiers or hardware information
- No location data
- No usage analytics or telemetry
- No crash reports sent to us
- No cookies, fingerprinting, or tracking of any kind

---

## Network Access

**None.** Our apps do not make any network requests. They function entirely offline.

- AstroBlinkV2 reads FITS and XISF image files from your local disk or network volumes that you explicitly select
- AstroFileViewer reads FITS and XISF image files that you explicitly open via the file picker or share sheet

Neither app connects to the internet for any purpose.

---

## File Access

Both apps only access files that **you explicitly choose** to open:

- **AstroBlinkV2** accesses folders and files you select via the Open dialog (Cmd+O). It may move files to a `PRE-DELETE` subfolder within the same directory when you use the pre-delete feature. No files are ever permanently deleted by the app.
- **AstroFileViewer** accesses individual files you open via the file picker, share sheet, or file association. When you use the "Save to Photos" feature, the app writes a JPEG copy to your Photo Library — this requires your explicit permission via the iOS photo library prompt.

No file contents are transmitted anywhere.

---

## Photo Library (AstroFileViewer only)

AstroFileViewer can optionally save a stretched JPEG version of an opened FITS/XISF image to your Photo Library. This requires your explicit permission via the standard iOS dialog. The saved image stays on your device — it is never uploaded or transmitted.
